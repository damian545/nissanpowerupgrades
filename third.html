Power and Cooling Upgrades:

Radiator, intercooler and Oil Cooler:



When generating a lot more power than factory power, your cooling system will fail eventually. Don't upgrade when it does fail, do it long before that happens. A 3 inch alloy radiator is best, something designed for your car so it bolts straight in. I love the factory looking upgrades, so it's not so obvious that it is modified. Your oil will thin out with lots of power too, so a 13 row or 15 row oil cooler and filter relocation kit will be best. High power also means hot inlet temperatures, so an intercooler kit is needed as well. Some Nissans have already been modified with an intercooler kit for you, unless you bought a stock car because they are in better condition. Better upgrade that stock fuel pump, that is a weak link to getting more power. When replacing all of these, make sure you get someone who knows how to getting working right. You will create new problems if it's done wrong, always get a professional to do the work for you.
